Ailish Roughan

Contemporary Jeweller

Jewellery has the ability to 'transform'. Not only in the making process from concept to design, metal to object, but how something so small, intimately and intricately made, becomes a part of the larger whole of the body. Each piece created becomes an outward expression of the self, from a quirky sense of humour to the inner ponderings of the soul!

My influences stem from childhood memories of growing up in the lush wilderness of the South Island west coast of New Zealand and my fascination of the colourful magic of fantasy and the dark mystery of fairytales.

Since graduating in sculpture from Elam school of fine arts in Auckland New Zealand, I have pursued jewellery studies in Auckland, Christchurch and Paris, France. Since the Christchurch earthquakes in 2010 I have been studying Fashion Design, and discovered a new creative passion! I exhibit my work in various venues in New Zealand.

My time overseas has made me value New Zealand even more. As a registered Eco Artist a percentage of sales of my work contribute directly to a NZ wildlife conservation fund.
